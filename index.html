The Best Man to Ever Live

Stoicism takes the position that the wise, the good man, the philosopher is a man who lives in accordance with nature. He fears only abdicating his moral responsibility. He is not afraid of pain. He is not afraid of death. He is not afraid of poverty. He is not afraid of any of the vicissitudes of the Human Condition. He fears only that he should let himself down and that he should be less than a complete human being.

— Dr Michael Sugrue

Stoic Recipes for a Better Life

Against Hardships

The vicissitudes of life will inevitably come knocking. It is useless to hope for an easy life.

Instead, follow this recipe if you face any of the following:

  • loss
  • poverty
  • illness
  • failure

Against Evil

Evil deeds are perpetrated by people from all walks of life. It is fruitless to hope to avoid them.

Instead, follow this recipe if you face any of the following:

  • ignorant
  • wicked
  • careless
  • well-meaning

Against Worry

The future might or might not come for you. It is pointless to fret over it or try to prevent it.

Instead, follow this recipe if you dread the following:

  • the coming days
  • the coming weeks
  • the coming months
  • the coming years

Against Death

Death is an integral part of life. It is the final duty of any and all living things. It is wasteful to dread it.

Instead, follow this recipe if you fear the following:

  • returning to dust
  • resting in peace
  • kicking the bucket
  • death

Who was Marcus Aurelius?

Marcus Aurelius, a Roman emperor and philosopher, became the ruler of Rome in 161 AD, serving until his death in 180 AD. Recognized as one of the “Five Good Emperors,” he reigned over the last part of the Pax Romana, or Roman Peace. Throughout his rule, Marcus Aurelius faced challenges, including military campaigns against Germanic tribes and the Parthian Empire.

Despite the the precedent of decadence and hedonism, he maintained a reputation for integrity and self-discipline, embodying Stoic principles in both his personal and political life.

Meditations: The Wisest Man's Diary

Meditations is a series of personal writings by Marcus Aurelius, providing a profound glimpse into his inner thoughts and philosophical reflections. Composed as a form of self-examination and guidance, the work is a collection of stoic wisdom that explores themes of virtue, resilience, and the nature of existence.

Divided into twelve books, Meditations offers a unique perspective on leadership, ethics, and the challenges of life. Written in a practical and straightforward style, the emperor-philosopher's diary contemplates the transient nature of human experience and the importance of living in accordance with reason and virtue.

The philosophical insights presented in Meditations have transcended time, making the work a timeless source of inspiration for individuals seeking wisdom and guidance in navigating the complexities of life. Marcus Aurelius' reflections continue to resonate with readers across the centuries, making Meditations a classic in the realm of philosophical literature.

Journaling: Becoming Marcus Aurelius

Ryan Holiday, a prominent author of stoic philosophy, considers journaling a pilar of stoicism. Holiday encourages individuals to engage in regular self-reflection, to examine their thoughts and actions, and to learn from their experiences.

Journaling, in this context, serves as a way to practice and learn stoicism. Holiday claims that philosophy isn't something we read once and understand. Rather, it is an active practice and something one does with oneself.
