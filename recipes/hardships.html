Against Hardships

According to Marcus Aurelius we should not fear external hardships such as pain, death, poverty, or the various challenges of the human condition. Instead, our primary fear should be abdicating moral responsibility and falling short of being a complete human being.



He acknowledges that negative events and adversities are inevitable in life. Marcus advises against futile efforts such as praying for negative events not to happen. He suggests accepting that these challenges will occur because nothing cane be done to prevent them. The focus, according to Marcus, should be on how to react to these events.



When adversity comes, Marcus Aurelius encourages a Stoic response. He advises against complaining and instead advocates for action and endurance. He reminds us that individuals can control their reactions to events, even if they can't control the events themselves.

Ingredients

  • Knowledge that adversity will inevitably come
  • Wisdom
  • Courage
  • Temperance
  • Justice

Steps

  1. Encounter hardships
  2. Determine what can be done
  3. Do what can and must be done
  4. Follow the four virtues
